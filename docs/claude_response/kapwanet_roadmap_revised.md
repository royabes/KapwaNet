# KapwaNet Roadmap: Through the Lens of Kapwa

*Not features, but expressions of philosophy*

---

## The Orienting Question

Before every feature, ask:

> **"Does this help people remember they belong to each other?"**

If the answer is no, don't build it.

---

## What We Got Wrong Before

The previous roadmap prioritized:
- Rich text editors
- Image upload
- Version history
- SEO metadata
- Approval workflows

These are **CMS features for power users**. They serve administrators, not community.

The research told us:
> "If it takes 10 minutes to post, people won't post."
> "Technology is 20% of the solution."
> "No explanation needed."

We weren't listening.

---

## The New Priorities

### Priority 1: Remove Shame from Asking

**The problem**: People suffer in silence because asking feels like failure.

**What to build**:

1. **Anonymous posting option**
   - Request help without revealing identity
   - Moderator can still contact, but community sees "A neighbor needs..."
   - Based on: Little Free Pantry principle

2. **"No explanation needed" toggle**
   - Remove the description field if user prefers
   - "I need [category]" is enough
   - Based on: Lasagna Love policy

3. **Reframe the request language**
   - Not "I need help" but "I'm inviting help"
   - Not "Request" but "Community opportunity"
   - Every request is a gift—the gift of letting others give

4. **Request celebration**
   - Celebrate new requests, not just completed ones
   - "Maria invited the community to help with groceries"
   - Flip the shame: asking is contributing

**The test**: Would my proudest, most independent friend feel okay using this?

---

### Priority 2: Remove Ego from Giving

**The problem**: Giving can become performance, savior complex, or transaction.

**What to build**:

1. **No hero leaderboards**
   - No "top givers" lists
   - No badges for volume of giving
   - Based on: Maimonides' teaching that anonymous giving is higher

2. **Reciprocity visibility**
   - "You've received 3 times and given 2 times"
   - Not to guilt, but to remind: this is a flow, not a direction
   - Everyone moves between giving and receiving

3. **Thank privately, not publicly**
   - Gratitude is personal, not performance
   - No public "thank you" wall that becomes performative
   - Private message is enough

4. **Giving as participating**
   - Language: "Participate" not "Help" or "Donate"
   - "Join this community moment"
   - You're not rescuing; you're showing up

**The test**: Could someone give without anyone knowing, and still feel complete?

---

### Priority 3: Make Asking Take 30 Seconds

**The problem**: Every field is friction. Friction kills participation.

**What to build**:

1. **One-tap request templates**
   - "I need groceries" → tap → done
   - Pre-filled categories with smart defaults
   - Details are optional, not required

2. **Voice posting**
   - "Tell us what you need" → speak → transcribe
   - Especially for elderly, low-literacy, busy parents

3. **SMS/WhatsApp integration**
   - Text a need to a number
   - Reply to confirm and post
   - No app download required

4. **Photo-first posting**
   - Take a photo → "What do you need?"
   - Broken appliance, empty fridge, flat tire
   - Picture worth 1000 words

**The test**: Can someone post a need while waiting for a bus?

---

### Priority 4: Impact That Inspires, Not Competes

**The problem**: Metrics can become competition. Competition kills community.

**What to build**:

1. **Community impact counters**
   - "This month: 47 meals shared, 12 rides given, 8 hours of childcare"
   - Collective, not individual
   - Based on: Olio's approach

2. **Story sharing (opt-in)**
   - "Share what this meant to you" → published anonymously
   - Not "look what I did" but "look what we did"
   - Human stories, not statistics

3. **Seasonal rhythms**
   - "Winter giving: 340 warm coats found homes"
   - Tied to community life, not arbitrary time periods
   - Celebrate collective response to collective need

4. **No individual rankings**
   - Never show who gave most
   - Never gamify generosity
   - Based on: Every wisdom tradition's warning about ego in giving

**The test**: Does this metric celebrate *us* or create *winners and losers*?

---

### Priority 5: Coordinator Care

**The problem**: The most caring people burn out. They give until empty.

**What to build**:

1. **Burnout indicators**
   - Track coordinator activity, flag overload
   - "You've handled 15 requests this week. Can someone else take the next one?"
   - Gentle, not judgmental

2. **Load distribution**
   - Auto-suggest backup coordinators
   - "Rotate" button to pass the torch
   - No single point of failure (or exhaustion)

3. **Coordinator appreciation**
   - Private thanks from other coordinators
   - Sabbath mode: "I'm offline this week"
   - Celebrate stepping back, not just stepping up

4. **Simple dashboard**
   - One screen: open needs, recent activity, who's available
   - Not a CRM, not analytics—just clarity
   - "What needs attention right now?"

**The test**: Can a coordinator take vacation without the community collapsing?

---

### Priority 6: The Unnamed Ones

**The problem**: Some people will never download an app, never post publicly, never admit need until crisis.

**What to build**:

1. **Intermediary posting**
   - "I know someone who needs..." → post on their behalf
   - Trusted community members as bridges
   - Based on: How help actually flows in many communities

2. **Physical touchpoints**
   - QR codes on flyers: "Scan to ask for help"
   - Partner with food banks, churches, clinics
   - Based on: Little Free Pantry's physical presence

3. **Phone hotline (future)**
   - Call to request, no technology needed
   - Volunteer-staffed, posts on behalf of caller
   - For the elderly, unhoused, tech-averse

4. **Outreach, not just intake**
   - Coordinators trained to notice who's struggling
   - "Haven't seen Maria at church. Let me check in."
   - Technology supports, not replaces, human noticing

**The test**: Can the person with no phone, no email, no comfort with technology still receive help?

## What We're NOT Building

1. **Social features** - No feeds, likes, comments, profiles. We're not a social network.

2. **Gamification** - No points, badges, levels. Generosity is not a game.

3. **AI matching** - No algorithms deciding who deserves help. Humans connect humans.

4. **Growth hacking** - No viral loops, referral bonuses, engagement optimization.

5. **Monetization** - No ads, no premium tiers, no selling data. Ever.

## The CMS Question

**Is the page editor even important?**

The previous roadmap focused on CMS power features. But:

- Organizations need to **run** mutual aid, not build fancy websites
- A simple landing page may be enough
- Time spent on CMS is time not spent on the help flow

**Revised CMS priorities**:

1. ✅ Basic page editor (done)
2. ✅ Template selection (done)
3. ✅ Theme customization (done)
4. ⏸️ Rich text editor (nice to have, not urgent)
5. ⏸️ Image upload (nice to have, not urgent)
6. ❌ Version history (not needed)
7. ❌ Approval workflows (not needed)
8. ❌ SEO optimization (premature)

**Focus instead on**: The help request/offer flow, coordinator tools, and removing friction.

## Metrics That Matter

**Not these**:
- Daily active users
- Posts per day
- Time on site
- User growth rate

**But these**:
- Needs fulfilled
- Time from request to resolution
- Participation depth (giving AND receiving)
- Coordinator burnout rate
- Anonymous requests (more = less shame)
- First-time askers (reaching new people)
